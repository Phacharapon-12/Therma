Abstract

Therma proposes a paradigm shift in Large Language Model (LLM) inference by discarding the computationally rigid, globally normalized Softmax layer. Instead, we introduce a Discrete Thermodynamic Machine (DTM) emulation utilizing JAX. By conceptualizing the pre-trained lm_head projection weights as an energy landscape, token generation is recast as a physical relaxation process within an Energy-Based Model (EBM). This framework demonstrates that autoregressive inference can be sustained on highly stochastic, analog-inspired architectures, paving the way for ultra-low-power edge deployment where thermal noise is harnessed as a computational utility rather than an adversarial artifact.

1. Zero-Shot Energy Matrix Projection

In standard digital inference, the hidden state vector is multiplied by the vocabulary projection matrix to yield logits, which are then exponentially normalized. Therma bypasses this exactness. We perform a zero-shot alignment, treating the projection weights as energy potential coefficients.

For a given hidden state $h_t$ and projection matrix $W$, the energy $E$ of a prospective token $x_i$ is defined natively as:

$$E(x_i) = - \left( h_t \cdot W_i \right)$$

The system samples the subsequent token by naturally relaxing toward low-energy states, governed by the Boltzmann distribution:

$$P(x_i) \propto \exp\!\left(-\beta\, E(x_i)\right)$$

This fundamentally removes the need for a complete partition function ($Z$) calculation across the entire vocabulary space, unlocking immense efficiency gains for hardware implementations.

2. The 512-Node Dual-TSU Ping-Pong Architecture

A primary bottleneck of MCMC and Gibbs sampling approaches is the sequential mixing time required to reach thermal equilibrium. To achieve competitive throughput, Therma implements a temporal multiplexing strategy via a Dual Thermodynamic Sampling Unit (TSU) architecture.

The 512-node master PGM is partitioned into two physical units. While TSU-0 is actively reading out the sampled token (Exploitation), TSU-1 is simultaneously injected with the next hidden state to begin its thermal relaxation phase (Exploration). This ping-pong sequence hides the stochastic settling latency entirely, maintaining a continuous inference pipeline.

Fig. 2 — Dual-TSU Ping-Pong Timing Diagram (live simulation)

The system Hamiltonian governing the inter-node coupling follows an Ising model, where each node $s_i$ is influenced both by its neighbors and by the external energy field from the transformer hidden state:

$$H(\mathbf{s}) = -\sum_{i,j} J_{ij}\, s_i s_j - \sum_i h_i s_i$$

The coupled energy of each node $k$ is then:

$$\tilde{E}_k = E_k^{\text{base}} + \underbrace{J \cdot m}_{\text{context coupling}}, \qquad m_t = \alpha\, m_{t-1} + \mathbf{e}_{k^*}$$

where $k^*$ is the node selected at the previous step and $\alpha \in (0, 1)$ is a decay factor, providing implicit coherence across tokens without an explicit attention mechanism.

3. Hardware-Aware Emulation: Quantization & Noise Tolerance

To validate the architectural viability for future analog substrates, the JAX-accelerated emulation strictly enforces simulated physical constraints. This ensures the algorithm operates robustly within defined error margins.

DAC Resolution Limits

The energy states are subjected to strict bit-width quantization (e.g., 6-bit to 8-bit precision), emulating the resolution bottlenecks of Digital-to-Analog Converters on physical chips:

$$E_q = \frac{\operatorname{round}\!\left(E_{\text{norm}} \cdot 2^N\right)}{2^N} \cdot \Delta E + E_{\min}$$

Thermal Noise Floor

Controlled Gaussian noise $\mathcal{N}(0,\,\sigma^2)$ is injected to model Johnson–Nyquist thermal fluctuations present in physical circuits:

$$E_{\text{eff}}(x_i) = E(x_i) + \epsilon, \qquad \epsilon \sim \mathcal{N}(0,\,\sigma^2)$$

Room-for-Error Allocation

Rather than suppressing analog noise, the system actively utilizes it to drive the Gibbs sampling stochasticity, proving that strict deterministic precision is not a prerequisite for coherent semantic generation.

4. Conclusion & Implications

The Therma proof-of-concept establishes that massive language models (Qwen2.5-0.5B) can seamlessly operate on physics-based sampling engines without requiring extensive re-training. By shifting the computational burden from digital exactness to thermodynamic relaxation, this architecture outlines a scalable blueprint for the next generation of analog AI hardware.

  • Zero re-training overhead: Pre-trained weights are repurposed directly as the energy landscape via a single transposition — no gradient updates required.
  • Hardware noise as signal: Thermal fluctuations drive Gibbs sampling stochasticity rather than degrading it, inverting the traditional engineering trade-off.
  • Latency hiding via ping-pong: Dual-TSU interleaving masks thermodynamic settling time, enabling continuous token throughput compatible with real-time inference.
  • Graceful quantization degradation: DAC bit-depth reduction produces predictable quality trade-offs, confirming the architecture's tolerance for analog imprecision.
By demonstrating that coherent autoregressive generation survives — and in principle benefits from — physical stochasticity, Therma provides an empirical foundation for thermodynamic processors as first-class inference accelerators.
